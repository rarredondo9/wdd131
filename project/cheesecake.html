Raspberry Cheesecake

Smooth and creamy cheesecake with a tangy raspberry topping. A dessert that's as beautiful as it is delicious.

Ingredients

    For the Crust

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1 1/2 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons melted butter

    • Filling

  • 2 packages (8 oz each) cream cheese, softened
  • 2/3 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

    • Raspberry Topping

  • 2 cup frozen raspberry
  • 3-5 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon lime
  • Fresh raspberries for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Line the bottom of your springform pan with a parchment paper round for easy removal.

    2. Crust

  2. Use a food processor to make graham cracker crumbs. Alternatively, crush them in a resealable bag using a rolling pin or kitchen mallet.
  3. Mix the crumbs with the melted butter, sugar, and salt. It should resemble wet sand and hold its shape when pressed.
  4. Add the crumbs to the pan and firmly pat down to get an even layer on the bottom and sides.
  5. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes. Set aside.

    6. Filling

  6. Reduce the oven to 325°F.
  7. Using a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment or a hand mixer, beat the softened cream cheese, eggs, sugar, sour cream, vanilla, and salt until smooth and creamy on medium-high speed.
  8. Scrape down the sides and bottom halfway through.
  9. Lastly, remove large air bubbles by reducing the speed to the lowest setting for 1 to 2 minutes.

    10. Water Bath

  10. Pour the batter into the springform pan and remove any air bubbles by banging the pan onto the kitchen counter lined with a kitchen towel.
  11. Put the springform pan into a larger roasting pan and add hot water a few inches up the side to create a water bath.

    12. Bake

  12. Bake at 325°F for 75 minutes.
  13. Turn off the oven and use a wooden spoon to crack open the oven door.
  14. Let the cheesecake slowly reduce in temperature for one hour then remove it from the oven.

    15. Chill

  15. Remove the cheesecake from the water bath and remove the foil.
  16. Once it reaches room temperature, loosely cover and chill in the fridge for at least 4 hours to overnight.

    17. Raspberry Topping

  17. To make the sauce, add the fresh or frozen raspberries, sugar, and lemon juice to a pot and heat over medium-high heat.
  18. Use the back of a spoon or masher to break down the raspberries.
  19. Once it starts to boil, turn off the heat.
  20. Taste and adjust with more sugar or lemon juice as needed.
  21. Cover and let it cool. Keep in mind the sauce thickens as it cools.

    22. Add raspberry topping and serve. ENJOY!
